Panthera

Species:

Panthera uncia

Traits:

  • whitish to gray fur with black spots on head and neck
  • cannot roar
  • eyes are pale green or grey in color

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Panthera tigris

Traits:

  • muscular body with powerful forelimbs
  • vertical black strips on fur
  • mane-like heavy growth of fur around the neck and jaws and long whiskers

Conservation Status: Endangered

Panthera leo

Traits:

  • short, rounded head
  • muscular and deep-chested
  • tail ends in a dark, hairy tuft

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Panthera onca

Traits:

  • largest cat that naturally occurs in South America
  • coat is generally a tawny yellow
  • adept at climbing, crawling, and swimming

Conservation Status: Near Threatened

Panthera pardus

Traits:

  • white-tipped tail
  • soft and thick fur
  • skin color varies by climate and habit

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Panthera spelea

Traits:

  • very thick and dense undercoat
  • males lack manes
  • smaller than the American lion

Conservation Status: Extinct
